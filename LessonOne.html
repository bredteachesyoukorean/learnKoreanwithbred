Welcome to the first ever Korean lesson in this series. When learning Korean, you obviously must be able to read it. and to read a language, you need to know the alphabet. Which is why, the first thing you must learn in Korean, is the Korean alphabet, or Hangul (한글)

Consonants:

Just like in English, there are not very many letters. In fact, there are less than in English. To be exact, 24 Korean letters are in 한글 (the Korean alphabet; pronounced "hangeul")

ㄱ - This letter is the same as the English "g". It often sounds like a "k" though. Another thing to remember, is that when ㄱ is combined with some other letters, it will look like this "가." You have not learned ㅏ yet, but just know that the g can be curved as well.

ㄴ - This letter is the same as the English "n". It always sounds like "n". This one will never become curved like ㄱ does.

ㄷ - This letter is the same as the English "d". It almost always sounds like a "d" but sometimes it is pronounced more like a "t".

ㄹ - This letter is the same as the English "L". It will sometimes sound like an l, and sometimes an r, but most of the time it will sound like an l. Also, remember that with asian languages, l often sounds like "d" or a tongue roll, but this will most likely come naturally.

ㅁ - Most likely one of the easiest to remember -- The same as the English "m". Like ㄴ, it will always sound like an "m".

ㅂ - This letter is the same as the English "b". It will occasionally sound like the letter "p" but you can almost always confidently pronounce this as a "b".
